Mind Map Component: Distinctions (The "Comparing Concepts" Branch)

This section contrasts related physical quantities and types of motion, highlighting their key differences.

Distance vs. Displacement

Basis of Distinction Distance Displacement
Definition Represents the **Total path length** covered. Represents the **Shortest path** measured from initial to final position.
Quantity Type A **Scalar** quantity (only magnitude). A **Vector** quantity (magnitude and direction).
Value Always **≥ 0** (Cannot be zero or negative). Can be **zero** (if final position = initial position) or negative.

Speed vs. Velocity

Basis of Distinction Speed Velocity
Definition Rate of change of **distance**. Rate of change of **displacement**.
Quantity Type A **Scalar** quantity. A **Vector** quantity.
Required Data Only **magnitude** is specified. **Magnitude** and **Direction** are specified.

Uniform Motion vs. Non-Uniform Motion

Basis of Distinction Uniform Motion Non-Uniform Motion
Distance/Time Covers **Equal distance** in equal intervals of time. Covers **Unequal distance** in equal intervals of time.
Velocity Velocity is **constant** (or zero acceleration). Velocity is **variable** (or non-zero acceleration).

Uniform vs. Non-Uniform Acceleration

Basis of Distinction Uniform Acceleration Non-Uniform Acceleration
Rate of Change Velocity changes by **equal amounts** in equal intervals of time. Velocity changes by **unequal amounts** in equal intervals of time.
